Malaysia's Socioeconomic Insights (1980 - 2016)

This comprehensive dataset presents an interesting socioeconomic overview of Malaysia from 1980 to 2016. These insights are derived from meticulous annual data collection, made publicly available by the Government of Malaysia. The dataset delves into critical aspects of the country's well-being, such as "Population Density, Unemployment Rate and Crime Rates" which have been carefully analysed for each of Malaysia's distinct local states.

Socioeconomic statistics in Malaysia vary from each states between the East Malaysia and West Malaysia, where the States that has the highest average of "Population Density" (Penang("1248.55")) and "Crime Rates" (Selangor("793.85 / 10,000 people")) lies in the West Malaysia. In the other hand, East Malaysia holds the lowest average of both "Population Density"(Sarawak("16.16")) and "Crime Rates"(Sabah("210.13 / 10,000 people")).

Statewise Crime Trends

Crime statistics in Malaysian states have been fluctuating over the years. Notably, Selangor consistently reported the highest crime rates. However, a positive trend emerged in 2007, with crime rates declining in most states. This shift towards safer communities is aligned with improved law enforcement strategies, community policing, public awareness, and collaborative efforts.
