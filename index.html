Malaysia's Socioeconomic Story
(1980 - 2016)

Population Density across Malaysia states

This comprehensive dataset shows an interesting socioeconomic overview of Malaysia from 1980 to 2016. These insights are derived from meticulous annual data collection, made publicly available by the Government of Malaysia. The dataset delves into critical aspects of the country's well-being, such as "Population Density" which have been carefully analysed for each of Malaysia's local states below.

**NOTE: PURPLE states means the data is unavailable.

Population Density statistics in Malaysia vary from each states between the East Malaysia and West Malaysia, where the States that has the highest Population Density (Penang) and lies in the West Malaysia. In the other hand, East Malaysia holds the lowest Population Density (Sarawak).

Unemployment Rate and Crime Rate Correlation

**NOTE: You have the option to filter the chart by selecting the STATES legend

The chart above illustrates the Unemployment Rate and Crime Rates correlation. It appears that states with higher unemployment rate tend to have higher crime rates, although this relationship is not very strong. Population Density is also a factor in the visualization, and it's worth noting that states with better crime rates do not necessarily have lower population densities. This suggests that the key to maintaining lower crime rates may depend on factors such as education, job opportunities, and government initiatives aimed at addressing these issues

Statewise Crime Trends

Crime statistics in Malaysian states have been fluctuating over the years. Notably, Selangor consistently reported the highest crime rates. However, a positive trend emerged in 2007, with crime rates declining in most states. This shift towards safer communities is aligned with improved law enforcement strategies, public awareness, and collaborative efforts.

Top Population States

Selangor has held the title of the most populous state in Malaysia since 1986, reflecting its status as a significant hub for population growth, likely driven by economic opportunities and urbanization. In contrast, Perlis has consistently had the lowest population among all states from 1980 to 2016, possibly due to its small land area, which may have limited its ability to attract a larger population compared to larger, more economically vibrant states like Selangor.

Decline in Unemployment Rate

The connected dot plot illustrates the changes in Malaysia's unemployment rate over the years 1980 to 2016. The plot shows how the unemployment rate has changed over time. In this case, we could see that most of the states unemployment rate shows a decline trends. This shows that most of the states have lower unemployment rate over time, and Melaka has the highest decline of unemployment rate over time (4.5% in 1980 to 0.9% in 2016). Even though, some of the states has their unemployment rate increased, Sabah holds the biggest unemployment rate increase over time (2.6% in 1980 to 5.4% in 2016).
